The Token Flow ▾

Creditor

Deposits collateral into a lending market but doesn't borrow against it. Stakes it in Twyne to make the unused borrowing power available.

Borrower

Pulls the Creditor's borrowing power into their vault, combining it with their own collateral to take on more debt.

Capital Efficiency

A standalone 99% liquidation threshold market would be costly to bootstrap. Twyne builds on top of the underlying market’s liquidity — it only needs to supply the incremental credit required to bridge the gap to the target LT.

Take stETH: Aave already provides ~95% LT. Twyne only needs to bridge the last ~4% — just 4 cents per dollar of borrower collateral. That's a 25x capital multiplier. All borrowable liquidity from the underlying market comes for free. This recomposes underlying protocol infrastructure into a new primitive — bootstrapping risk-segmented markets at a fraction of the cost.

Risk & Security

Two layers of liquidation: an internal mechanism that causes zero creditor losses, and an external fallback that is safe in most cases but carries tail risk.

We stress-tested the worst case — assuming no internal liquidations fire and every position falls back to external liquidation. Annualized creditor loss: ~0.16% at 94% max LT. Creditors are compensated for this risk through the delegation fee. Importantly, this was volatile pairs only — correlated pairs with hardcoded oracles carry a fundamentally different risk profile.

What's Next

In TradFi, clearinghouses like DTCC function as netting layers for money and credit. DeFi followed the same arc: AMM aggregators evolved from simple pool routing into layers like CoWSwap, where orderflow is matched before touching liquidity.

Twyne becomes the single entry point for lending — user flows are netted, risks are segmented and turned into opportunities. In the short term, we're working on:

Extend Credit Lines

Credit delegation routes borrowing power on Aave to any asset. Yield-bearing stablecoins can permissionlessly onboard to Aave. On top of that, and more uniquely, it unlocks time-delayed operations: Redemption Markets, RWA settlement, cross-lending market netting.
